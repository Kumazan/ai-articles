# Family & Group Travel (Core Rule 6)

## Travelers File

Create `trips/{trip-name}/travelers.md` for any group or family trip:

```markdown
## Travelers — {Trip Name}

| Name | Age | Dietary | Medical | Seat Pref | Notes |
|------|-----|---------|---------|-----------|-------|
| {name} | {age} | {e.g., vegetarian} | {e.g., nut allergy} | aisle/window | |
```

## Planning Checklist

- [ ] Create `travelers.md` with each person's details (dietary, medical, seat preferences)
- [ ] Plan kid-friendly activities with energy/nap breaks between
- [ ] Book accommodations with kitchen access (saves 30%+ on food costs)
- [ ] Check child visa / parental consent requirements
  - Some countries require notarized consent letters for minors traveling without both parents
  - Countries known to enforce: South Africa, Mexico, Canada border crossing
- [ ] Build a shared items list to avoid duplication (first aid kit, power adapters, etc.)
- [ ] Assign roles among adults:
  - **Navigator** — routes, transport, maps
  - **Budget tracker** — receipts, expense logging
  - **Activity planner** — reservations, tickets, confirmations

## Accommodation Tips

- Request adjoining or connecting rooms at time of booking, confirm again 48h before arrival.
- Apartments/vacation rentals with kitchens are almost always better value for 4+ people / 5+ nights.
- Check for crib/rollaway availability when traveling with infants.

## Activities

- Alternate high-energy and low-energy activities each day.
- Always have a free/indoor backup for bad weather.
- Ask venues about family/group discounts before purchasing.
- Book timed-entry tickets for popular attractions — avoid queues with young children.

## Flight & Transport

- Book seats together when booking flights — don't rely on airline to group at check-in.
- For 2+ adults: consider splitting into 2 bookings for flexibility (one delay doesn't cancel all).
- Ground transport: compare 2 medium cars vs 1 large van — often cheaper and more flexible.
