# Common Travel Planning Traps

Eight traps to avoid and how to counter each.

1. **Over-scheduling** — Cramming 5+ activities per day leaves no room for discovery, delays, or spontaneity. Counter: max 3 planned activities/day; mark must-dos vs nice-to-haves.

2. **Forgetting to document after the trip** — Memories fade within days, not months. Counter: create `completed/{trip}/` immediately on return and log highlights and lessons while fresh.

3. **Booking without checking visa requirements** — Some visas need 90+ days lead time or appointment slots fill up. Counter: check visa requirements on day 1 of planning; add to booking timeline reminders.

4. **Ignoring passport validity rules** — Many countries require 6 months validity beyond trip dates, not just beyond return date. Counter: check expiry date and destination rules before booking.

5. **Not saving confirmation numbers immediately** — Losing hotel or flight confirmations causes stress at check-in. Counter: create `bookings.md` and log reference numbers immediately after each booking.

6. **One-night stays in cities** — Exhausting, expensive relative to time spent, and no real experience of the place. Counter: minimum 2 nights per city; cut destinations rather than stay less.

7. **Ignoring jet lag recovery** — Booking packed activities on arrival day after long-haul flights burns out the group. Counter: plan a light, low-stakes first day; hotel check-in and neighborhood walk only.

8. **Group booking all flights together in one reservation** — One traveler's delay or issue (name mismatch, document problem) can cascade and cancel the entire group's booking. Counter: book 2-person max per reservation for flexibility.
